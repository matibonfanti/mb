Interesting Reads

Papers, books, essays I find interesting, insightful and relevant

Tech and AI

  • Leopold Aschenbrenner's "Situational Awareness: the Decade Ahead" - an ex-OpenAI employee's perspective on the future outlook of AI. Disagree with his risk assessment and geopolitical suggestions, but it's a deep dive by an insider.
  • "How LLMs work" by 3Blue1Brown - a visual intro to understanding transformer models.

Economics and Markets

  • Mandelbrot's "The (Mis)Behavior of Markets: A Fractal View of Risk, Ruin, and Reward" - challenges Gaussian distributions and efficient markets, applying fractal geometry to markets. For technical details, see "The Variation of Certain Speculative Prices" (1963) and "A Multifractal Model of Asset Returns" (1997).
  • Rothbard's "The Mystery of Banking" - a detailed explanation (and critique) of how the banking system works.
