interesting reads

papers, books, essays i find interesting, insightful and relevant

tech and ai

  • Leopold Aschenbrenner's "Situational Awareness: the Decade Ahead" - an ex-openai employee's perspective on the future outlook of ai. very much disagree with his risk assessment and his strategic geopolitical suggestions but it's a (somewhat technical) deep dive by a very smart insider.

  • "How LLMs work" by 3Blue 1Brown - a visual intro to understanding transformer models.

  • Answering the Call of Automation: How the Labor Market Adjusted to Mechanizing Telephone Operation - labor market adaptation in response to automation. complimenting this: Artificial Intelligence, Scientific Discovery, and Product Innovation - proves ai is already having very significant productivity and labour saving impacts highlights interesting skill-based disparities.

  • "Cultural Evolution of Cooperation among LLM Agents" by Google Deep Mind - a super interesting paper on how "societies" of LLM models develop cooperative behaviors over generations in a Donor Game. Claude 3.5 wins!

economics and markets

  • Mandelbrot's "The (mis)Behaviour of Markets: a Fractal View of Risk, Ruin, and Reward" - challenges the assumptions of gaussian distributions and efficient markets. applies fractal geometry to markets, shows that they exhibit self-similarity. for more technical stuff see "The Variation of Certain Speculative Prices" (1963) and "A Multifractal Model of Asset Returns" (1997).

  • Rothbard's "The Mystery of Banking" - a detailed and historically accurate explanation (and critique) of how the banking system works.

  • "Political Cycles and Stock Returns" - develops a model linking political cycles and stock returns to time-varying risk aversion, showing that higher stock market returns under Democratic presidents are driven by high risk aversion during their elections, rather than their policies.

  • the Baumol effect - how productivity gains in one sector raise costs in less productive sectors.

investing

  • Seth Klarman's "Margin of Safety" - value investing with a focus on exploiting market inefficiencies, maintaining a contrarian mindset, and prioritizing preservation of capital. (find on Libgen or emial me for a pdf)

  • "Intelligence and Global Bias in the Stock Market" - uses a log-normal model to reveal how biases, driven by managerial discretion and accounting practices, systematically distort stock prices from their intrinsic values. identifies stable proxies for true fundamentals.

  • Warren Buffett's "The Superinvestors of Graham-and-Doddsville" - arguing against the efficient market hypothesis by demonstrating that disciplined investing in undervalued securities consistently outperforms the market.
